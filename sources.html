Source guide

Use this page to understand what each source can help you find and when to use it.

Start with the Universal Search

The Universal Search already uses these source paths behind the scenes. This page explains what each source is for so families understand why a result matters.

Search everything

Family identity records

Use for: names, tribe/Nation, Dawes roll number, census card number, family group, parents, and enrollment category.

  • Oklahoma Historical Society Dawes search: a quick way to look for a name, tribe, roll number, or card number.
  • NARA Dawes records: Final Rolls, enrollment/census cards, applications, allotment jackets, and maps.
Open OHS Dawes searchOpen NARA Dawes guide

Land-location records

Use for: township, range, section, map page, allotment number, legal description, nearby names, roads, rivers, and townsites.

  • AllottedLand.com map index: reviewed site records and map-page leads as they are added.
  • Library of Congress maps: original map images and atlas pages.
  • BLM/GLO: survey plats, field notes, tract books, patents, and township land catalog leads.

What happened to the land later

Use for: deeds, mortgages, releases, probate, guardianships, tax deeds, sheriff deeds, oil/gas leases, court cases, rights-of-way, and title history.

  • County clerk and court records: the local title and court trail.
  • BIA/LTRO: trust/restricted title documents, patents, deeds, probate orders, leases, rights-of-way, plats, and title-status direction.
  • Historic newspapers: tax-sale, sheriff-sale, probate, guardianship, and local legal notices.
  • Federal Register: BIA notices, land acquisitions, rules, and federal-source leads.

Verification rule

Use these sources to find leads, then verify with the original record. Do not rely on a search result alone for title, enrollment, heirship, ownership, or legal claims.
