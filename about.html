What this site is

About AllottedLand.com

A free research guide that helps Native families turn names, places, roll numbers, map clues, and family stories into a record trail.

What AllottedLand.com does

The site helps families figure out what to search next. Put the information you have into the Universal Search. The site builds a research path, shows matching site records when they exist, and points to official source systems such as NARA, OHS, LOC maps, Federal Register, newspapers, Census/TIGERweb, county records, BIA/LTRO, and BLM/GLO.

Start the Universal Search

What families can enter

  • Ancestor or family name, including spelling variants and married names.
  • Tribe/Nation, Dawes roll number, census card number, or allotment number.
  • Township, range, section, county, old town, cemetery, creek, or road.
  • Book/page, case number, deed, mortgage, probate, tax sale, sheriff sale, or oil/gas information.
  • A modern address or map pin when the family only knows a current place.

What this site cannot prove by itself

AllottedLand.com is not a court, county clerk, BIA/LTRO, NARA, tribal enrollment office, title company, or law firm. A result is a lead. The proof is the original record chain.
