About AllottedLand.com

A free public research tool that helps Native families turn family information into a record trail for Dawes records, allotment jackets, maps, county records, BIA/LTRO records, and land-history research.

What this site does

AllottedLand.com helps families decide what record to look for next. Search can organize names, roll numbers, card numbers, township/range/section, county information, addresses, deeds, tax-sale information, probate information, and family stories into a research path.

Search results are leads. The proof comes from original source records such as Dawes cards, enrollment packets, allotment jackets, maps, county deed records, court files, BIA/LTRO title records, and other official records.

What this site does not do

AllottedLand.com does not decide title, ownership, heirship, enrollment, citizenship, trust status, restriction status, mineral rights, probate rights, or legal rights. It is not a tribal, federal, county, court, title-company, or law-firm system.

Contact

Use email for questions, corrections, removal requests, volunteer coordination, or site problems.

Email: allottedland@gmail.com

Helpful information to include

  • The page, result, source link, or record involved.
  • Ancestor or family name, tribe/Nation, roll number, card number, township/range/section, county, or case/book/page information if relevant.
  • Whether you are asking for a correction, removal, volunteer help, or general guidance.

Do not email sensitive private documents. Do not send Social Security numbers, private addresses of living people, government IDs, financial records, medical records, or unredacted private family documents.
