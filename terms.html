Terms of Use

Use this site as a research guide. Results are leads, not legal proof.

Last updated: June 28, 2026. Updated in v0.52 for free outside resource links and future partner disclosures.

Research leads only

AllottedLand.com helps users find possible records, source links, and research paths. It does not decide land ownership, title, heirship, citizenship, enrollment, probate rights, mineral rights, trust status, restriction status, or legal rights.

Verify everything

Search results may be incomplete, unverified, user-submitted, OCR-derived, or based on partial information. Verify every match against original records and official offices before relying on it.

Responsible use

  • Do not submit private information about living people without permission.
  • Do not impersonate another person.
  • Do not submit information you know is false.
  • Do not treat site results as legal advice.

Free for Native families

AllottedLand.com is intended to remain free for families using it to find allotment, Dawes, map, county, BIA/LTRO, and land-loss research leads. Optional outside resources may appear, but using this site does not require buying a subscription or paying AllottedLand.com.

Outside resources and partner links

The site may link to free outside genealogy resources, including FamilySearch, to help families gather names, dates, relatives, and places before searching allotment records. Outside websites are not controlled by AllottedLand.com and may have their own terms, privacy rules, account requirements, and record restrictions.

FamilySearch is shown as a free resource in this version, not as a paid affiliate link. If paid partner or affiliate links are added later, they must be labeled clearly near the link. Free official and free genealogy sources should remain first.

Independent project

AllottedLand.com is independent. It is not an official tribal, federal, BIA, NARA, county, court, FamilySearch, or title-company system.
